Antenna Positioning.

Arm 01 — Physical world interface. Controls the servo and stepper system that moves the antenna array to the exact position the head requests, and confirms it got there.

01

What it does

Physical control at agent speed

Arm 01 is the mechanical effector of the system — the only arm that changes something in the physical world. It receives a positioning directive from the head, computes the required servo and stepper movements, executes them through the PCA9685 PWM controller over I²C, and reports back with encoder-verified confirmation.

The arm doesn't just fire and forget. It holds a closed feedback loop: after every move, it reads the encoder position and compares it against the commanded target. If the delta exceeds the configured tolerance (default ±0.5°), it issues a correction pulse and waits. This loop runs up to three times before the arm escalates to the head with a positioning fault.

Antenna inventory and swap sequencing

The arm maintains a local manifest of available antennas — their physical slot positions, frequency range, gain profile, and last-used timestamp. When the head requests a swap, arm 01 checks whether the target is already mounted. If it is, it confirms without moving. If not, it plans a multi-step sequence: retract the active antenna to a safe park azimuth, rotate to the target slot, engage the locking mechanism, extend, and verify connection continuity via a simple RF loopback test before reporting success.

This sequencing is critical. Swapping antennas while arm 02 is mid-observation would corrupt the IQ data. Arm 01 never initiates a swap during an active observation window — it holds the request until the head confirms arm 02 has closed its capture session.

Tracking mode

For astronomical targets that move across the sky, arm 01 supports continuous tracking mode. The head provides azimuth/elevation coordinates and an update rate (typically 1 Hz for most targets, up to 10 Hz for LEO satellites). The arm maintains a micro-step movement buffer, smoothing jitter between coordinate updates. Slew rate is limited to prevent mechanical resonance in the mount, and the arm reports actual vs commanded position on each update cycle.

The physical layer. Arm 01 connects the agent swarm to the real world — one of only two arms that interact with hardware you can touch.

Status

  • State: Active
  • Protocol: MCP over stdio
  • Hardware bus: I²C at 400kHz
  • Update rate: Up to 10 Hz tracking
  • Position accuracy: ±0.5° after correction
  • Energy draw: 8–35W (idle to slew)

Hardware

The physical stack.

PCA9685 PWM controller

16-channel, 12-bit PWM driver over I²C. Drives up to 4 servo motors simultaneously at 50 Hz. The arm uses channels 0–3 for azimuth, elevation, and locking mechanism. I²C address is configurable via solder bridges — default 0x40. Powered from a dedicated 5V rail, logic from 3.3V.

NEMA 17 stepper + A4988 driver

The main azimuth axis uses a NEMA 17 stepper motor (1.8°/step, 2A rated) driven by an A4988 microstepping driver at 1/16 step resolution — giving 3200 steps per revolution and 0.1125° theoretical precision. The driver is thermally managed; arm 03 monitors its rail and throttles step rate before the driver hits its thermal limit.

AS5048A magnetic encoder

14-bit absolute magnetic rotary encoder mounted on the azimuth shaft. SPI interface at 10 MHz. Provides 0.022° resolution — well inside the ±0.5° tolerance budget. The arm reads encoder position via SPI after every movement sequence to close the feedback loop. No homing required on startup; absolute position is always known.

RF continuity probe

A simple SMA loopback fixture and a 1 Hz RF tone from arm 02's SDR confirm antenna connection after every swap. The probe measures insertion loss on the active antenna port; values outside the expected range trigger a swap verification failure and re-seat attempt.

Wiring summary

  • Raspberry Pi 5 → PCA9685: I²C SDA/SCL + 3.3V + GND
  • PCA9685 → Servos: PWM signal + 5V + GND per channel
  • Pi SPI0 → AS5048A: MOSI, MISO, SCLK, CE0
  • Pi GPIO → A4988: STEP, DIR, ENABLE, MS1–MS3
  • A4988 → NEMA17: 2A bipolar coil pairs (A1/A2, B1/B2)
  • Power: 12V@3A for stepper, 5V@2A for servos (separate rails)

MCP Tools

The interface.

swap_antenna(target: string, verify: boolean = true)

Swap the active antenna to the named target from the arm's inventory manifest. If verify is true, runs the RF continuity probe after mounting. Blocks until the sequence completes or a fault is raised.

Returns: { ok: bool, position_deg: float, insertion_loss_db: float, duration_ms: int }
Example: swap_antenna("yagi-1420mhz", true)
Faults: position_timeout, continuity_fail, slot_empty
get_position()

Read the current azimuth and elevation from the AS5048A encoder without issuing any movement command. Used by the head to verify state before dispatching an observation.

Returns: { azimuth_deg: float, elevation_deg: float, timestamp_ms: int }
Example: get_position()
Latency: <5ms (direct SPI read)
slew_to(az: float, el: float, rate: string = "normal")

Move the antenna mount to the given azimuth/elevation. Rate can be "slow" (0.5°/s), "normal" (2°/s), or "fast" (5°/s). Fast rate is gated by arm 03 energy budget approval. Returns when target is reached within tolerance.

Returns: { ok: bool, final_az: float, final_el: float, delta_deg: float }
Example: slew_to(180.0, 45.0, "normal")
Faults: range_limit, motor_fault, energy_denied
track(az: float, el: float, rate_hz: float = 1.0)

Begin continuous tracking mode. The head sends updated coordinates at rate_hz; the arm buffers and smooths them into micro-step sequences. Returns immediately; tracking continues until stop_track() is called.

Returns: { tracking: bool, buffer_depth: int }
Example: track(272.4, 31.8, 1.0)
Max rate: 10 Hz (LEO satellite tracking)
list_antennas()

Return the full antenna inventory manifest — all configured antennas, their slot positions, frequency ranges, gain values, and availability status.

Returns: Array<{ id, slot, freq_min_mhz, freq_max_mhz, gain_dbi, mounted: bool }>
Example: list_antennas()
park()

Move the antenna to the configured safe park position (default 0° azimuth, 0° elevation). Used during power transitions and fault recovery. Always succeeds unless the motor is physically blocked.

Returns: { ok: bool, position_deg: float }
Example: park()
Called by: head on shutdown, arm itself on fault

Failure modes

What breaks.
How it recovers.

position timeout

Motor moves but encoder doesn't confirm within 3s

The arm retries the correction pulse twice. If encoder still disagrees after three attempts, it issues a park command, marks the current position as unknown, and reports position_fault to the head. Head suspends observations until arm 01 is re-homed by the operator.

continuity fail

RF probe detects high insertion loss after swap

Arm 01 retracts and re-seats the antenna once. If continuity still fails, it reports to the head with the measured insertion loss value. Head may request a different antenna from the inventory rather than retrying.

motor fault

A4988 thermal shutdown or overcurrent

Arm 03 should catch this first via rail monitoring. If it doesn't, arm 01 detects loss of step acknowledgment and immediately disables the driver. Parks on the last known good encoder position using servo-only movement. Reports motor fault with thermal context to head.

I²C bus hang

PCA9685 stops responding

The arm issues an I²C bus reset (SCL toggling sequence). If the device doesn't recover within 500ms, it reports a hardware fault and stops accepting commands. All servos hold their last position (PWM continues from the PCA9685's internal register).

Integration

How it fits.

Integration contract

Receives from
Head only. Positioning directives, swap requests, tracking coordinate updates.
Reports to
Head only. Position confirmations, fault events, antenna inventory state, energy requests (forwarded via arm 03).
Context needed
Antenna manifest (local). Arm 02 observation state (via head — never direct). Energy budget approval for fast slew (via arm 03 through head).
Produces
Verified position readings. Swap completion events with insertion loss data. Tracking status at configured rate.
Dependencies
Arm 02 must not be in active capture. Arm 03 must approve energy budget for fast operations. No other arm dependencies.
